## üìù Getting Started

### 1. Submit Your Topic

Use the [submission form](../../meetings/templates/submission-form.md) or contact the session host directly.

**Good Topic Examples:**
- "How I automated our design handoffs with Claude"
- "5 ChatGPT prompts that save me 2 hours daily"
- "Building a content generator with OpenAI API"
- "AI-assisted code review: what works and what doesn't"

**Topics to Avoid:**
- Pure theory without practical application
- Company-confidential information
- Sales pitches for external tools
- Topics requiring extensive setup

### 2. Choose Your Format

| Format | Duration | Best For | Preparation |
|--------|----------|----------|-------------|
| Lightning Talk | 5-10 min | Tips, tools, quick wins | 1-2 hours |
| Feature Demo | 15-20 min | Workflows, tutorials | 3-5 hours |
| Clinic Topic | 5 min intro | Problems, discussions | 30 min |

### 3. Plan Your Content

**Lightning Talk Structure:**
- Hook (30 sec): Why should people care?
- Context (1 min): What problem does this solve?
- Solution (3-5 min): Show the tool/tip/workflow
- Takeaway (1 min): How can others apply this?

**Demo Structure:**
- Setup (2 min): What we're building/showing
- Live demo (10-12 min): Step-by-step walkthrough
- Debrief (3-5 min): Lessons, gotchas, next steps

## üõ† Preparation Best Practices

### Content Development

**Start with the "Why"**
- What problem does this solve?
- Who benefits from this knowledge?
- What's the real-world impact?

**Keep it Practical**
- Show real examples, not toy demos
- Include actual code/prompts/workflows
- Address common issues and solutions

**Make it Actionable**
- Provide links and resources
- Give clear next steps
- Share templates or starter code

### Technical Preparation

**Demo Requirements:**
- Test everything beforehand
- Have backup plans for live demos
- Prepare screenshots if demo fails
- Use realistic data and examples

**Resource Sharing:**
- Prepare all links in advance
- Create a demo repository if needed
- Have code snippets ready to share
- Test all shared resources work

### Timing and Practice

**Time Management:**
- Practice with a timer
- Plan for interruptions and questions
- Leave buffer time for technical issues
- Have a short version ready

**Rehearsal Tips:**
- Practice the demo flow multiple times
- Test screen sharing and visibility
- Rehearse transitions between sections
- Prepare answers to likely questions

## üìã Day-of Presentation

### Pre-Session Checklist

**30 minutes before:**
- [ ] Join the Teams meeting early
- [ ] Test screen sharing and audio
- [ ] Have all browser tabs and tools ready
- [ ] Share your links in the chat

**During your slot:**
- [ ] State your name and topic clearly
- [ ] Share your screen when ready
- [ ] Keep slides minimal - focus on live demo
- [ ] Encourage questions throughout
- [ ] Share resources and links in chat

### Presentation Tips

**Engagement:**
- Start with a relatable problem
- Use "we" language instead of "I"
- Ask the audience questions
- Encourage experimentation

**Technical Delivery:**
- Make text large and readable
- Speak to your screen sharing, not local view
- Pause for questions at natural breaks
- Highlight mouse cursor for clarity

**Handling Questions:**
- Repeat questions for remote attendees
- It's okay to say "I don't know"
- Offer to follow up after the session
- Direct complex questions to open clinic

## üöÄ Advanced Tips

### Making Your Demo Memorable

**Show the Journey:**
- Start with the raw problem/input
- Show the AI interaction in real-time
- Reveal the final output/solution
- Explain your thought process

**Include Failures:**
- Show what doesn't work and why
- Demonstrate how you debug issues
- Share lessons from failed experiments
- Normalize the iteration process

**Make it Interactive:**
- Ask for input from the audience
- Let them choose demo parameters
- Take live requests during demos
- Encourage them to try it themselves

### Building Community

**Connect to Previous Sessions:**
- Reference related past presentations
- Build on others' shared knowledge
- Acknowledge inspiration from teammates
- Create learning progressions

**Encourage Follow-up:**
- Offer to pair program with interested folks
- Suggest related topics for future sessions
- Start team experiments or challenges
- Share your learning resources

## ‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What if I'm not an AI expert?**
A: Perfect! We want diverse perspectives. Share what you've learned, no matter your experience level.

**Q: Can I present something I'm still learning?**
A: Absolutely. Learning journeys are often more valuable than finished expertise.

**Q: What if my demo breaks during the presentation?**
A: Have screenshots ready, explain what should happen, and use it as a learning moment.

**Q: How technical should I get?**
A: Match your audience. Explain concepts clearly and offer to go deeper in the clinic.

**Q: Can I present with a colleague?**
A: Yes! Pair presentations can be great, especially for different perspectives on the same tool.

**Q: What if I run over time?**
A: The host will give time warnings. Have a shortened version ready to wrap up quickly.
